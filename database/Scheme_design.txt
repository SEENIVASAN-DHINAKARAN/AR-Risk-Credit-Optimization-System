------------------------------------------------------------------
AI-DRIVEN ACCOUNTS RECEIVABLE RISK & CREDIT OPTIMIZATION SYSTEM
SCHEMA DESIGN DOCUMENT
------------------------------------------------------------------

1. PROJECT OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------
Project Name:
AI-Driven Accounts Receivable Risk & Credit Optimization System

Purpose:
To simulate an ERP system for Distributors, Dealers, and Hospitals,
and build a predictive Accounts Receivable (AR) risk model to reduce
Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) and optimize credit limits.

Data Scope:
Synthetic ERP data will be generated for:
- Customers
- Invoices
- Payments

The system will later use this data for:
- Machine Learning risk prediction
- Credit limit optimization
- KPI monitoring
- Executive dashboard reporting


2. BUSINESS ASSUMPTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------

1. Credit Term = 60 days
2. Cost of Capital = 12% annually
3. Customer Risk Distribution:
   - Low Risk = 40%
   - Medium Risk = 35%
   - High Risk = 25%
4. Delay Patterns:
   - Low Risk: -5 to +2 days (early or on-time payment)
   - Medium Risk: 5 to 15 days delay
   - High Risk: 20 to 45 days delay
5. Data Period: Last 12 months
6. Estimated Customers: 400
7. Estimated Invoices: 10,000+
8. One invoice will have one payment for simplicity.


3. TABLE STRUCTURES
--------------------------------------------------------

--------------------------------------------------------
TABLE 1: customers
--------------------------------------------------------
Description:
Stores master details of Distributors, Dealers, and Hospitals.

Columns:

1. customer_id        VARCHAR(20)  PRIMARY KEY
2. customer_type      VARCHAR(50)  (Distributor / Dealer / Hospital)
3. region             VARCHAR(50)  (North / South / East / West)
4. credit_limit       DECIMAL(15,2)
5. risk_profile       VARCHAR(20)  (Low / Medium / High)

Note:
risk_profile is used internally to generate realistic payment behavior.
It will NOT be used in dashboard or final reporting.


--------------------------------------------------------
TABLE 2: invoices
--------------------------------------------------------
Description:
Stores invoice transaction details.

Columns:

1. invoice_id         VARCHAR(20) PRIMARY KEY
2. customer_id        VARCHAR(20) FOREIGN KEY REFERENCES customers(customer_id)
3. invoice_date       DATE
4. due_date           DATE
5. invoice_amount     DECIMAL(15,2)

Business Logic:
Due Date = Invoice Date + 60 days


--------------------------------------------------------
TABLE 3: payments
--------------------------------------------------------
Description:
Stores payment transaction details linked to invoices.

Columns:

1. payment_id         INT AUTO_INCREMENT PRIMARY KEY
2. invoice_id         VARCHAR(20) FOREIGN KEY REFERENCES invoices(invoice_id)
3. payment_date       DATE
4. payment_amount     DECIMAL(15,2)

Business Logic:
Payment behavior depends on customer risk_profile.


4. RELATIONSHIPS
--------------------------------------------------------

1. One Customer → Many Invoices
2. One Invoice → One Payment

Relationship Mapping:

customers.customer_id  → invoices.customer_id
invoices.invoice_id    → payments.invoice_id


5. DATA LOGIC NOTES (FOR ML & KPI STAGE)
--------------------------------------------------------

1. Delay_Days = Payment_Date – Due_Date
2. Delay_Flag = 1 if Delay_Days > 0 else 0
3. Credit Utilization = Outstanding / Credit Limit
4. Average Delay will be calculated at customer level
5. Late Payment Frequency will be calculated per customer
6. Logistic Regression will be used to predict delay probability
7. Risk Categorization:
   - 0–30%  → Low Risk
   - 31–60% → Medium Risk
   - 61–100% → High Risk
8. Credit Recommendation:
   - High Risk → Reduce credit limit by 30%
   - Medium Risk → Maintain current credit limit
   - Low Risk → Increase credit limit by 15%
9. Expected Loss = Probability of Default × Exposure
10. Working Capital Blocked = Outstanding × Cost of Capital
